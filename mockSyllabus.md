This is a comprehensive breakdown for **German, French, and Spanish**. To fit a **50-hour curriculum** per sub-level, each sub-level is divided into **10 Lessons (5 hours per lesson)**.

Each 5-hour lesson typically includes: 2 hours of core grammar/vocabulary, 1 hour of listening/speaking, 1 hour of reading/writing, and 1 hour of review/assessment.

---

### **B1-B2: Intermediate/Advanced**
*   **B1 (100 hrs):** Mastery of *Subjonctif* (all uses), *Plus-que-parfait*, logical connectors (*alors que, bien que*), and argumentative writing.
*   **B2 (100 hrs):** Focus on *Mise en relief*, the "Passé Simple" (literary), debating ethics, French politics, and professional synthesis.

### **A2: Elementary**
| Sub-Level | Lesson (5 hrs each) | Topic & Grammar Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A2.1** | 1-2 | Biographies, Preterite Indefinite (Advanced/Irregulars). |

### **B1-C2: Proficiency Track**
*   **B1 (100 hrs):** Heavy focus on Subjunctive (Desire, Doubt, Emotion), *Pretérito Pluscuamperfecto*, and the "Se" impersonal.
*   **B2 (100 hrs):** Imperfect Subjunctive, Conditional sentences (*Si clauses*), Passive Voice, and regional slang/idioms.
*   **C1-C2 (200 hrs):** Stylistic mastery, legal/medical terminology, analysis of Hispanic literature (García Márquez, Cervantes), and high-level diplomatic debate.
